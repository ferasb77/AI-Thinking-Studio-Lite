# Privacy and AI Use

This document provides the minimum responsible-use position for AI Thinking
Studio deployments. It should be reviewed against the actual hosting,
database, model-provider and client arrangements before public or client use.

## Participant-facing notice

AI Thinking Studio uses artificial intelligence to help participants examine
framing, assumptions, stakeholder perspectives, possibilities and potential
consequences. It does not make decisions or recommendations. AI-generated
material may be incomplete, inaccurate or shaped by the information provided.
Participants remain responsible for all judgments, decisions and actions.

Thinking sessions may be stored so that participants can return to their work
and prepare a Thinking Record. Participants should not enter confidential,
classified, personally identifiable or commercially sensitive information
unless they have authorization to process it through the deployed environment.

## Deployment decisions to complete

- Identify the legal entity acting as data controller.
- Record the hosting region and Supabase project region.
- Document which AI provider receives participant inputs.
- Define whether provider-side training or retention is disabled.
- Define session and account retention periods.
- Establish account-access, correction and deletion procedures.
- Establish incident response and participant notification procedures.
- Confirm facilitator and administrator permissions.
- Provide a named privacy contact.
- Obtain client approval when organizational information will be processed.

## Suggested footer

**AI supports examination; it does not replace human judgment.** Do not enter
sensitive information unless you are authorized to process it here. For access,
retention or deletion questions, contact feras@enablemygrowth.com.
